## Rationale

This paper is a cornerstone in the shift toward data-centric AI, proving that the composition and instructional quality of a dataset—resembling a student's textbook rather than just a massive internet dump—can enable smaller models to achieve state-of-the-art performance. For a technical decision-maker, this paper is highly actionable; it provides a blueprint for replacing or augmenting expensive, massive-scale data collection with targeted, synthetic-data generation pipelines. Its significance lies in its direct attack on the scaling laws that dominated early LLM development, providing a tangible pathway for smaller, more efficient teams to achieve competitive performance in specialized domains like code generation.
