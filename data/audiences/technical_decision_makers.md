---
audience_id: technical_decision_makers
name: Technical Decision-Makers in AI
description: >
  Practitioners who directly influence AI investment and build decisions —
  heads of applied AI, staff and principal ML engineers, AI product leads,
  and technical PMs in AI-heavy orgs. They have enough technical depth to
  evaluate a paper or dataset claim, and enough authority to act on it.
persona: >
  A senior practitioner who reads deeply when a signal directly affects a
  current build or resource decision, and skims or ignores everything else.
  They have seen hype cycles before and trust concrete technical evidence
  over framing. They are time-constrained: a briefing that does not reach
  the "so what" in the first paragraph loses them. They value honest
  uncertainty over false confidence.
scope: >
  Their allocation decisions span: which research to adopt, which data
  collection bets to fund, which infrastructure to prototype, and which
  external datasets or methods to track for future integration. They are not
  pure researchers (they need to ship) and not pure product managers (they
  need technical substance). The brief should sit at that intersection.
tone: >
  Direct. Technical but not jargon-heavy. Confident about what is known and
  explicit about what is uncertain. No cheerleading. If something is
  overhyped, say so. If the evidence is weak, say so. Treat the reader as a
  peer, not a student.
